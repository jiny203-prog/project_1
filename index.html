I see myself as a dead person, watching that day coldly, perhaps the passing of the last day and the arrival of night. Comprendí que el libro era monstruoso, que era una cosa imposible, y que violaba las leyes de la realidad. I thought to myself that a person can become someone else’s enemy, someone else’s enemy for a period of time, but cannot become an enemy of a region, fireflies, words, gardens, water currents, and winds. Este libro se llama el Libro de Arena, porque ni el libro ni la arena tienen principio ni fin. With relief, with humiliation, with terror, he understood that he too was a mere appearance, dreamt by another.
